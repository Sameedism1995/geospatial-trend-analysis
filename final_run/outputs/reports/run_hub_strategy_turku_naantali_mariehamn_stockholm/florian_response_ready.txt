Subject: revised hub strategy — addressing your concern

Hi Florian,

I revised the hub strategy after your feedback so that local port
impact and regional background are no longer mixed.

1. Turku and Naantali are ~15 km apart on the same Finnish coast,
   so I combined them into a single Turku-Naantali coastal hub.
   Treating them separately had been double-counting the same
   coastal/industrial system.

2. Mariehamn is retained as an independent island/offshore hub,
   used as the low-urban-background contrast.

3. Stockholm is added as a spatially distinct urban port hub.
   This lets us test whether the near-port NO2 signal is really
   urban/atmospheric rather than ship-source: if Stockholm shows
   a clear 0-50 km NO2 increase but Mariehamn does not, NO2 is
   acting as an urban regional signal.
   Caveat: the existing 315-cell grid was built around
   Mariehamn / Turku / Naantali and the nearest grid cell is
   ~81 km from Stockholm.  The 0-50 km Stockholm test is
   therefore INCONCLUSIVE in this dataset; I report Stockholm
   50-200 km as the closest substitute and flag it explicitly
   in no2_interpretation_check.md.  A follow-up run with a
   Stockholm-extended grid is the natural next step.

4. Port-level interpretation is now restricted to 0-100 km only,
   with a 25 km sliding window and 10 km step.

5. Distances of 100-300, 300-500 and 500-1000 km are reported only
   as regional background bands; every regional figure title says
   'REGIONAL BACKGROUND PATTERN - NOT PORT IMPACT' so the
   thesis cannot misread them as port effects.

All outputs for this revised analysis are saved under
outputs/reports/run_hub_strategy_turku_naantali_mariehamn_stockholm/,
outputs/figures/run_hub_strategy_turku_naantali_mariehamn_stockholm/, and
outputs/visualizations/run_hub_strategy_turku_naantali_mariehamn_stockholm/.
Previous runs are preserved for comparison.
