# Figure 5.9 — Temporal lag structure

Weekly panel medians are standardised across the pooled period, then smoothed with a centered five-week rolling mean so multi-week anomalies stand out beyond week-to-week noise. Year–month annotations mark pooled extreme tails (approximately the top ~1–2 percent of peaks in pooled absolute smoothed scores). Lag-autocorrelation cells report Spearman rank correlation between median series at offsets of one and two weeks (entries with near-zero correlations across both lags are omitted from the plotted matrix). Contemporary Spearman correlations use the same weekly medians; coefficients with absolute value below 0.14 are blanked everywhere except on the diagonal to stress structurally coherent relationships rather than negligible drift. Persistence condenses standardized rolling-five-week-medians of NO2 and MEI into one composite index; shaded bands highlight sustained episodes above the pooled 88th percentile of that index.
