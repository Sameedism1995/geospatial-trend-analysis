ESI WEIGHTING SENSITIVITY CHECK
================================
Method: Component z-scores identical to build_indices() in
  src/analysis/run_coastal_exposure_analysis.py (weekly z-scores for
  local_no2_excess, vessel_density_t, ndti_weekly_anomaly, oil_slick_probability_t).

Input: processed/features_ml_ready.parquet + wind merge + prepare_panel() + build_indices()
Panel size: 16,065 grid-week rows (thesis panel)

Primary ESI (sensitivity): rank-percentile of equal-weight mean of the four z-scores (25% each).
Alternative ESI: rank-percentile of weighted sum — 35% NO2 excess, 35% vessel density,
  15% NDTI weekly anomaly, 15% oil slick probability.

Comparison sample: rows where all four component z-scores are finite (n = 2,050).

RESULT (thesis panel)
---------------------
  Spearman rho = 0.920, p < 0.001, n = 2,050

Subsets:
  coastal_panel only (all 4 finite):     n = 208,  rho = 0.924
  Turku + Mariehamn ports (all 4 finite): n = 1,314, rho = 0.944

Note: Full pipeline environmental_stress_index also includes a wind-rank term in its
equal-weight mean (5 parts). On the same n = 2,050 overlap, pipeline ESI vs equal
4-component ESI: rho = 0.996 — the sensitivity paragraph compares the two 4-component
weighting schemes, not the full 5-part pipeline index.

THESIS WORDING (insert actual rho)
----------------------------------
To assess sensitivity to the equal-weighting assumption, an alternative ESI was
computed assigning 35% weight each to NO₂ excess and vessel density and 15% each
to NDTI anomaly and oil slick probability, reflecting MARPOL Annex VI regulatory
priorities. The Spearman correlation between the alternative and primary ESI was
ρ = 0.920 (p < 0.001, n = 2,050), indicating that temporal and spatial patterns
are robust to the weighting scheme. Equal weighting was retained for transparency
and reproducibility.
